**Library Facial Recognition Kiosk System**  
Effective Date: **March 8, 2026**  
Last Updated: **March 8, 2026**

## 1. Purpose

This Privacy Policy explains how the Library Facial Recognition Kiosk System ("System") collects, uses, stores, and protects personal data for identity verification and library access management.

By using this System, users acknowledge and agree to the practices described in this policy.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- Students, staff, and authorized users who register and use the kiosk
- Library and ICT personnel who administer the System
- Data processed through the web admin dashboard and kiosk interfaces

## 3. Data We Collect

The System may collect and process the following:

- Full name
- SR Code / institutional identifier
- Program / department
- Facial images captured during registration
- Face embedding vectors (numerical facial features used for matching)
- Recognition logs (date/time, confidence score, result)
- Staff account data (username, role, profile image, login activity)
- Audit trail of administrative actions

## 4. Why We Process Data

Your data is processed for legitimate institutional purposes, including:

- Identity verification at library kiosk points
- Access monitoring and attendance/usage analytics
- Fraud prevention and duplicate identity detection
- System security, diagnostics, and performance improvement
- Administrative accountability and incident investigation

## 5. Legal and Institutional Basis

Processing is performed in line with institutional security requirements and applicable data protection standards/policies.  
Data is limited to what is necessary for identity verification and operational management.

## 6. Data Retention

Data is retained only as long as needed for service delivery, security, and compliance:

- Registration records: while the user remains active in the institution, or until valid deletion request/administrative offboarding
- Recognition logs: based on institutional retention schedule
- Audit logs: retained for security review and accountability

Authorized administrators may remove or purge records through approved system functions when applicable.

## 7. Access Control and Security

The System enforces role-based access control:

- **Super Admin**: full administrative control
- **Library Admin**: operational administration
- **Library Staff**: limited operational access

Security measures include:

- Authenticated staff-only dashboard access
- Password hashing and session-based authentication
- Role restrictions on sensitive routes
- Audit logging of critical admin actions

No system is completely risk-free; however, reasonable technical and organizational safeguards are continuously applied.

## 8. Data Sharing

Personal data is not shared with unauthorized third parties.  
Data may be disclosed only when required by institutional policy, legal obligation, or authorized internal security/disciplinary processes.

## 9. User Responsibilities

Users must:

- Provide accurate registration information
- Avoid impersonation or unauthorized access attempts
- Follow institutional use and conduct rules

Misuse of this System may result in access suspension and disciplinary action under institutional policies.

## 10. User Rights

Subject to applicable rules and verification:

- You may request to review your personal records
- You may request correction of inaccurate data
- You may request deletion or deactivation when appropriate

Requests may be denied or limited if retention is required for legitimate security, legal, or institutional obligations.

## 11. Policy Updates

This policy may be updated to reflect system changes, legal requirements, or institutional directives.  
Updated versions will be posted through official system channels.

## 12. Contact

For privacy concerns, correction requests, or access/deletion requests, contact:
**Library Management**

Please include your full name, SR Code, and concern details for verification and faster processing.
